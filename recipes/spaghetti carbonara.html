Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish that epitomizes the beauty of Italian cooking - creating something extraordinary from just a few simple ingredients. The dish features silky strands of spaghetti coated in a luxuriously creamy sauce that's made without any cream at all, relying instead on the magical combination of eggs, cheese, and pasta water. The crispy bacon or pancetta adds bursts of salty, smoky flavor that contrasts beautifully with the rich, velvety sauce. What makes carbonara so special is the technique - the hot pasta literally cooks the raw eggs as you toss them together, creating a sauce that clings to every strand without scrambling. The result is incredibly indulgent yet surprisingly light, with freshly cracked black pepper adding a gentle heat that cuts through the richness. Carbonara is deceptively simple but requires perfect timing and technique, as you have only moments to combine the hot pasta with the egg mixture before it either becomes perfectly creamy or turns into scrambled eggs. When done right, it's comfort food at its finest - satisfying, elegant, and deeply flavorful.

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Eggs and Parmesan cheese
  • Bacon or pancetta

Steps

  1. Prepare the components
  2. Combine while hot
  3. Finish and serve
